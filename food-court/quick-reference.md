## Objective

Score the most Victory Points (VP). All VP come from served customers: each customer scores its base
value (1–4) plus bonus value covered by End Condition cards in tracking, mood/personality color 
matches (+2), and nationality matches (+1).

## Setup

1. Deck Selection: Each player selects one restaurant deck.
2. Customer Deck:
   - Remove all Customer Cards from the cuisine decks and combine them into a shared customer deck.
   - Shuffle, then reveal a central queue of face‑up customers sized (players + 1). Place the rest
     face down as a draw pile.
3. Your Station:
   - Place your Ability Card in front of you.
   - Place your deck face down as a draw pile; draw 6 cards.
   - Reserve space for a discard pile, End Condition tracking, an in‑play area (ingredients/recipes/
     utensils/drinks), and a scoring pile for served customers.
4. First Player: choose randomly. Turns proceed clockwise.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

1. Cook: 
   - Cook recipes using prepared ingredients
   - Play Drink Cards when their requirements are met.
2. Serve:
   - Select customers from the central queue and fully cover their base value
     with cooked recipes' total value. Move served customers to your scoring pile.
   - After you finish serving, refill the central queue if possible.
   - If you served 2 recipes with the same utensil symbol, you may put the matching Utensil Card
     into play.
   - If you met your special ability condition, you may put 1 End Condition card into tracking.

Outside your turn:
- Discard up to 3 cards, then draw up to your hand limit of 6.
- Prepare ingredients up to your Preparation Limit.

## Limits

- Preparation Limit: 2 (3 with Kitchenware).
- Cooking Limit: 2 (3 with Cookware).
- Serving Limit: 2 customers (3 with Tableware).
- Hand Limit: 6 cards.

## Card Types

### Ingredient Cards

Ingredients come in three types:
- Primary — Required in every recipe.
- Secondary — Required in normal and hard recipes.
- Optional — Increases that recipe’s serve value by 1.

### Recipe Cards

Recipes are cooked using prepared ingredients and provide serve value; they do not score by
themselves. Optional ingredients and some special abilities may add +1 serve value in specific
situations. Discard cooked recipes and their used ingredients after serving.

### Drink Cards

Play when their requirement is met to immediately move 1 customer from the central queue
to your scoring pile (serve without meeting their base value), then discard the drink.

### Customer Cards

Can be served drinks and recipes to be attracted to a pile for end‑game scoring. Scoring is from:
- Base Value (1–4): The serve value you must allocate in a single turn to serve that customer.
- Mood & Personality: If a served customer’s mood color matches the previous one's personality
  in your scoring pile, gain +2 VP.
- Bonus Value: Gain +1 VP for each End Condition card in tracking (up to the depicted amount of 
  medal symbols).
- Nationality: If it matches your deck, gain +1 VP.

### Utensil Cards

Each deck has Kitchenware, Cookware, and Tableware.
- Putting Into Play: If in one turn you serve 2 recipes with the same utensil symbol, you may put
  the matching Utensil Card into play.
- Ongoing Effects while in play:
  - Kitchenware: Preparation limit +1 (2 → 3).
  - Cookware: Cooking limit +1 (1 → 2).
  - Tableware: Serving limit +1 customer (2 → 3).

### Ability Card

Lists your deck’s unique ability, its End Condition, and how to track it. Special abilities are
evaluated only from served recipes, and a card cannot participate in more than one special ability
occurrence.

## Winning the Game

The game ends after the round in which a player puts 4 End Condition cards into tracking or the
customer deck is emptied. Finish the current round. Then each player calculates total VP from served
customers (base plus bonuses). Highest total wins.

Tiebreaker: the player who most recently cooked a real‑life meal wins.
